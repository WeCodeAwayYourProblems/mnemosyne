Matthis wondered how many more dawns he would see before he died. The peaks of Blackvein turned chilly blue that blocked the watery orange sun. Miners and bread makers rose first, but children reacted to the uncaged corpse first. A group of them charged toward him, saw what stood next to him, and shriveled. They whimpered and ran back to the square yelling, "Bone man, bone man!" aa though it were an alarm. 
Matthis closed his eyes. A stiff breeze knocked at him, threatened to bend him. He squares himself, resisted. He opened his eyes. His jaw clenched. 
A procession of about thirty adults spotted him and aimed themselves in his direction. The Vicar led the way, flanked by Mercy. Matthias spotted Rafe, Bartholomew and Godric the mining wizards, Corven the Miner's Guild master in his usual mustache, and Elspeth. The group formed a semicircle twenty feet away. 
Mercy shrieked, and he couldn't tell if she directed it at him or the undead wizard behind him.
